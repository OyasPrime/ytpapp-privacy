## 1. Information We Access or Collect

### Google Account Information

When you connect your YouTube Music / Google account, the app may access basic Google account information such as your name, email address, and profile picture for authentication and account connection purposes.

### YouTube Playlist Data

The app uses YouTube Data API services to read and update playlists that you choose to manage. This may include playlist titles, playlist IDs, video IDs, song or video titles, channel or artist information, thumbnails, durations, view counts, dates, and playlist item order.

### App Preferences and Local Data

The app may store basic settings locally on your device, such as selected language, premium status cache, ad unlock session state, and app preferences.

### Purchase and Subscription Information

If you use Premium features, Google Play Billing may provide purchase or subscription status information so the app can unlock Premium features.

### Advertising Data

The app uses Google Mobile Ads / AdMob to show rewarded ads. Google Mobile Ads SDK may collect or process device identifiers, advertising ID, app interactions, diagnostics, and related data for advertising, analytics, fraud prevention, security, and compliance.

## 2. How We Use Information

We use information only to provide app features, including:

- Signing you in or connecting your Google / YouTube Music account
- Reading your YouTube Music playlists
- Sorting and reordering playlist items
- Saving playlist changes back to YouTube
- Showing rewarded ads for free playlist unlocks
- Managing Premium access
- Remembering app settings such as language
- Improving app reliability and preventing abuse

## 3. Data Storage

YT Playlist Sorter does not operate its own backend server or database for storing your YouTube playlist data.

Some information may be stored locally on your device, such as app settings, cached status, or temporary session information. You can delete this local data by clearing the app data from your device settings or uninstalling the app.

Google, YouTube, Google Play, and AdMob may process data according to their own policies when their services or SDKs are used.
